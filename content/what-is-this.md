It's my personal website.

Here I talk about anything that comes to my mind, mostly things I've learned or projects I've created. 
Most of the content in this blog will be software/hardware related since that's what i mostly spend my time on.

## Who are you?
I'm João, I develop software and build random stuff sometimes.

## What topics do you talk about?
Software, self-hosting, Linux, decentralization, books, hardware hacking, things like Arduinos and Raspberry Pis, sometimes ML and LLMs, automations, personal analytics, command-line tools, [open-source](https://github.com/JoaoPito), physics, mechatronics, jailbreaks.

## What kind of software do you develop?
Mostly tools. My open-source projects are mostly on Python, C# or Golang. Although i avoid JS and TS, sometimes i build something using it.

Recently i started using Golang more often, mostly to build my TUI interfaces using [bubbletea](https://github.com/charmbracelet/bubbletea/tree/main).

Sometimes i make my way into uncharted territories for absolutely no reason, a side product of it is *[this website](/)*, which is made using [Hugo](https://gohugo.io/), or my personal analytics dashboard, which is make using [Grafana](https://grafana.com/).

## Where are you from?
I'm from Brazil.

## Is this website vibe-coded/AI-slop/[insert-your-LLM-related-buzzword-here]?
No. 

But i'm not exactly against vibe-coding, at least not in the sense of a magical prompt/new tool/agent that creates high-quality software from the ground up autonomously.

I believe that ""AI"" (LLMs) do provide great benefits if they are used within a proved and well-thought system. I can go in more details about my workflow with LLMs, but i generally avoid using them in an environment without version control and safeguards.

I use LLMs to assist on writing some of the articles for this website, but by no means the content here is AI-generated or (i hope so) slop. I believe that most people can detect if an article is AI-generated and get turned off by it, i'm one of these people and i wouldn't spend any more than 2 minutes in it, so i won't do it.
